# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Prosumers who handle PDFs seriously: researchers, students, analysts, developers, knowledge workers. They review documents professionally (annotating, commenting, tracking feedback), run toolbox operations (merge, split, compress, redact), and are actively annoyed by cloud-based tools that are slow, require uploads, or have paywalls. They want fast, local, private, and capable.

Primary scenario: a researcher or analyst is working through a 50-150 page document. They need to highlight, comment, tag questions, jump between annotations, and export a review summary. They prefer the keyboard to the mouse. They've used Acrobat or PDF Expert before and know what a real tool looks like.

Secondary scenario: a power user who occasionally needs to batch-merge PDFs, extract pages, compress for email, or convert images. They want the job done without uploading to a stranger's server.

## Product Purpose

A local, private PDF toolkit. Does what SmallPDF and Adobe do, but entirely on your machine. Two modes in one app: a serious document reviewer (annotation, comments, search, sidebar, export) and a toolbox of utility operations (merge, split, compress, watermark, rotate, extract, convert).

Success looks like: a user who would have opened Acrobat, or grudgingly gone to SmallPDF, instead reaches for this without thinking about it.

## Brand Personality

Warm precision. Confident, capable, unhurried. The tool that does the job without ceremony.

Three words: **precise, warm, capable**.

Voice tone: direct and clear. No marketing language, no hand-holding. Speaks to the user as a peer who knows what they're doing.

Emotional goal: the quiet satisfaction of a tool that respects your time.

## References

- **Highlights.app / Readwise**: focused reading tool warmth — clean typography, purposeful spacing, product someone actually loves
- **Linear**: precision-tool aesthetic — tight, fast, keyboard-native, every pixel justified
- **Anthropic / Claude**: confident restraint — distinctive choices, warm without being decorative, not generic-tech

## Anti-references

- **SmallPDF / iLovePDF**: rounded, cloud-service marketing look. Too friendly, too web-app.
- **Generic AI-generated interfaces**: default gradients, hero metrics, identical card grids, side-stripe borders. Zero character. If it looks like it was scaffolded in 30 seconds, it was. Avoid.
- **Adobe Creative Cloud dark mode**: heavy dark chrome, flashy, creative-pro aesthetic that doesn't fit a utility tool.

## Design Principles

1. **Privacy is character.** Everything runs locally — that's not just a feature, it's an identity. The UI should feel self-contained and trustworthy, never cloud-adjacent.

2. **Speed is respect.** Every interaction should feel instant. Latency is never hidden behind spinners when avoidance is possible. The keyboard gets you everywhere a mouse can.

3. **Warmth without softness.** Approachable human warmth (Highlights, Claude) combined with professional precision (Linear). Not cold utility. Not cute app. The tool a professional reaches for and doesn't feel embarrassed about.

4. **Distinctive over generated.** Make considered choices that show authorship. Break the first reflex (dark = serious, blue = tech, cards everywhere). The aesthetic should feel like it was designed, not deployed.

5. **The document is the hero.** Chrome serves content. Sidebars, toolbars, and panels earn their space by supporting the document, not decorating themselves. When the user is reading, the UI should recede.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Minimum contrast 4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. All interactive elements keyboard-navigable with visible focus indicators. Support `prefers-reduced-motion` for animations.
